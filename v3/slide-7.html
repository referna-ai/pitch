The mechanism

InTouch delivers all 3 required components

1
Find willing referrers
InTouch combines onboarding inputs with calendar, email, and messaging signals to identify each member's ICP — then matches them into circles whose businesses naturally refer to each other.
Early signal: 3.5 invites per user · 29% acceptance  ·  n=48
2
Keep them motivated
Circles of 3–8 commit to mutual lookout. Each member scans their meetings for the others; the others scan back. BNI's reciprocity loop — minus the geography and the breakfast. Motivation is structural, not exhortational.
BNI ran this offline for 40 years to $26.4B/year · 79% of members in firms under 10
3
Trigger recall at the right moment
Calendar is one input. InTouch maintains a collaborative CRM across the circle and predicts when a member's business is ready to be handed off. The signal doesn't go to the next vendor cold — it goes to the existing vendor, reminding them which circle-mate to refer in the meeting they're already having.
The inversion of intent platforms: attributed intent surfaced inside a trust loop
BNI proved the loop at $26.4B/year. InTouch makes it digital, ambient, and continuous.
